5 products that increase the level of hemoglobin

Hemoglobin is a protein that is present in red blood cells and carries oxygen from the lungs to all tissues of the body. It contains iron, the level of which as a result of various processes in the body can decrease, which leads to insufficient oxygen supply to the organs. A condition in which iron levels decrease is known as iron-deficiency anemia (IDA). Anemia is also sometimes caused by increased blood loss or simply insufficient iron intake from food. The main goal of the treatment of Alzheimer's disease is to increase iron intake, which in turn leads to an increase in hemoglobin levels.

What foods are useful for increasing hemoglobin levels?

1. Red meat.

Red meat is an excellent source of heme iron (easily digested by the body). Because this type of meat is also rich in fat-enhancing nutrients the risk of cardiovascular abnormalities, it is important not to overdo it.

2. Vegetables.

All green leafy vegetables are rich in iron. Vegetables that can be consumed with a low hemoglobin level: beets, spinach, cabbage, turnips, green peas, sweet potatoes, pumpkins, cauliflower. In any season on the market you can find some of these vegetables and include them in your menu. Perhaps the most useful of all these vegetables is beetroot, which activates red blood cells and improves the supply of oxygen to tissues.

3. Fruits.

Fresh fruits and even some dried fruits are very useful for people who suffer from low blood pressure gland. You can focus on raisins, prunes, apples, apricots, papayas, strawberries, grapes, and watermelon. In addition, fruits rich in vitamin C help to digest iron more easily in the body, so they are also recommended to add to the diet (oranges, grapefruit, lemon).

4. Whole grains products.

Look at the labels of all the wholegrain foods you buy. They should contain 20% of the daily iron (the norm is 10-30 mg depending on gender, age, etc.). This type of food is very useful, and people should try to include it in their daily menu.

5. Nuts.

Nuts they are also rich in iron. In this regard, the most useful of them is almonds. 30 grams of almonds a day give 6% iron.

If you have low hemoglobin levels, avoid foods that inhibit iron absorption by the body. These are coffee and foods rich in fiber.
