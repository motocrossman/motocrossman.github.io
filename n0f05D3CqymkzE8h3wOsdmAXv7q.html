Foods that are good for your teeth

Dental care is not limited to regular brushing of teeth. What a person is is especially important for their health. Most often, they are destroyed due to the accumulation of plaque as a result of the multiplication of bacteria in the body cavity. These bacteria feed on the sugar contained in carbohydrates, and secrete an acid that destroys tooth enamel. There are products that can improve the condition of your teeth and delay your visit to the dentist.

Fiber-rich foods.

This type of food has a massaging effect on the gums, promotes brushing of teeth and increases salivation. When the amount of saliva in the mouth increases, acids harmful to the teeth are neutralized. Raw fruits and vegetables should be an integral part of the menu if you want to have healthy teeth. However, you should be careful with the use of citrus fruits, since they are very acidic and can damage your tooth enamel. After eating these fruits, it is recommended to brush your teeth with toothpaste to remove acids that can accumulate in the mouth.

Foods containing calcium and phosphorus.

Calcium and phosphorus are the two main minerals that make up teeth. If for any reason the enamel is missing if your teeth are damaged, eating foods that contain these minerals can help restore some of the enamel and protect your teeth from damage. The best source of calcium is cheese, cottage cheese, and fresh yogurt. Phosphorus can be obtained from meat and fish.

Foods that need to be chewed more.

The teeth must "be in good shape to stay healthy. For this reason, it is recommended to eat foods that need to be chewed more. These are, for example, apples, carrots, celery.

Water.

Water, like saliva, helps to wash away acids and sugars that accumulate in the mouth. It also contains fluoride, a mineral that protects your teeth from erosions. This is one of the main ingredients of toothpastes.

Berries.

Strawberries contain malic acid. It acts as a natural bleaching agent. You can mix ground strawberries with baking soda and apply it on your teeth. After five minutes, rinse it off. After a few such procedures you will notice a positive effect.

In addition to these changes that need to be made to your diet, you should not stop using regular toothpaste and flossing to remove stuck food particles. In addition, you should go to the dentist at least twice a year for preventive examinations.
