4 types of alcohol addiction

In narcology, the concept of heterogeneity of alcoholism and the existence of separate subtypes of addiction is widely spread. Perhaps the most scientifically proven is the classification of 4 types of alcoholic beverages dependences.

Type I: "alcohol Intake as a result of biological (physical) attraction" or " avoidance model»;

Type II: "alcohol Intake due to mental attraction" or " anxiety or conflict pattern»;

Type III: "alcohol use as a cure for mental disorders» or "alcohol as an antidepressant»;

Type IV: "alcohol Intake due to an uncritical attitude to alcoholism in the culture of the environment, brain dependence on alcohol" or "alcohol as an adaptation".

In particular, type I is people with severe withdrawal symptoms who drink to relieve them. They have primary organic (biological) weakness, often fully adapted in society, but already affected by severe somatic and / or psychiatric withdrawal symptoms (e.g., alcoholic psychosis and / or epileptiform seizures). Complete abstinence from alcohol intake is the most appropriate therapy.

People with type II drink to achieve an anxiolytic effect. They have clear premorbid features, where the main factors are the relationship between parents and children and personal development. In this type, effective control over alcohol use can still be achieved, that is, complete abstinence from taking it is possible, although with periodic breakdowns.

The main feature of type III is affective disorder (most often bipolar affective disorder). Emotional and behavioral disorders lead to a fluctuating course of the disease with severe relapses.

Type IV - drinking with the typical premorbid characteristics. They are characterized by a poor climate in the family, minimal organic brain damage (of neonatal origin) and behavioral disorders, such as nocturnal enuresis (after 3 a.m.). The patients themselves to "cure" behavioral or physical symptoms alcohol.

This typology is carried out using specialized software based on a questionnaire, is mandatory in some centers around the world and is used in determining therapy from the moment of admission to treatment, because abstinence occurs differently in different types.

The results of studies using this typology show that from the point of view of forming a psychiatric diagnosis, it is useful as a parameter for successful prognosis and for therapeutic recommendations. Several therapeutic studies have shown that the prognosis largely depends on this typologies.

It is worth Recalling that the treatment of alcoholism and any of its types is carried out only by a specialist doctor.
